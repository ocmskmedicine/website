How It Works

Your Own Blood, Concentrated and Activated

PRP (platelet-rich plasma) is derived from a small sample of your own blood. Platelets and growth factors are concentrated using ExoCube, one of the newest PRP systems available, then activated so those growth factors are immediately available to the body.

The concentrated PRP is then delivered precisely to the site of injury or degeneration under real-time ultrasound guidance — ensuring it reaches the exact structure causing your pain.

The PRP process
1 A small blood sample is drawn, just like a routine lab draw
2 The sample is spun down to concentrate platelets and growth factors
3 The PRP is activated so growth factors are immediately available
4 The PRP is injected directly into the injury under real-time ultrasound guidance

Common Questions

How is your PRP different? +

We use one of the newest PRP systems available, yielding a higher concentration of platelets and growth factors than conventional PRP, with an activation step that makes growth factors immediately available — the same technology trusted by professional athletes.

Is PRP the same as stem cell therapy? +

No. PRP uses only your own concentrated platelets and growth factors — a safe, well-studied orthobiologic therapy. We do not offer unregulated peptide therapies or unproven "stem cell" treatments.

Why does ultrasound guidance matter? +

Blind injections frequently miss their target. Every PRP procedure at OCMSK is performed under real-time ultrasound guidance, so the injection is delivered to the exact structure causing your pain.

How many PRP treatments will I need? +

Many patients see meaningful improvement after a single treatment, thanks to our activated, high-concentration protocol. Some conditions benefit from a short series of injections — we'll outline a specific plan at your consultation.

Is this covered by insurance? +

We accept PPO plans through Cigna, Anthem Blue Cross, Aetna, and United Healthcare, as well as Medicare; coverage varies by treatment. Self-pay patients are welcome with transparent, accessible rates. Call us and we will help verify your benefits.

What happens at the first visit? +

A full history and physical exam plus a diagnostic ultrasound of the affected area — so you leave with an accurate diagnosis, an understanding of every factor contributing to it, and a clear treatment plan.
