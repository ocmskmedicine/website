Conditions This Includes

Diagnoses We Commonly Treat

Adhesive Capsulitis (Frozen Shoulder)

Progressive stiffness from thickening and tightening of the shoulder joint capsule.

Rotator Cuff Tendinopathy

Chronic irritation and degeneration of the rotator cuff tendons.

Biceps Tendinosis

Degeneration of the biceps tendon at the front of the shoulder, often coexisting with rotator cuff injury.

Partial Rotator Cuff Tear

An incomplete tear of one of the rotator cuff tendons.

Shoulder Impingement Syndrome

Pinching of rotator cuff tendons between the shoulder blade and upper arm bone.

Subacromial Bursitis

Inflammation of the bursa beneath the shoulder blade, often accompanying impingement.

This list is not exhaustive. We evaluate and treat many other musculoskeletal conditions — contact us to schedule a consultation for a proper diagnosis and treatment options.

Diagnosis First

An Accurate Diagnosis Is the Treatment That Matters Most

Shoulder pain and stiffness can come from a tight joint capsule, a torn or inflamed rotator cuff tendon, or both at once — and the treatment for each is different. Every evaluation at OCMSK includes a real-time diagnostic ultrasound exam of the shoulder, so we know exactly which structures are involved before anything is treated.

We also assess your range of motion, shoulder mechanics, and any prior injury, and build a plan that restores motion and function — not just reduces pain.

Common signs of frozen shoulder & rotator cuff injury
— Progressive loss of shoulder range of motion, especially reaching overhead or behind the back
— Pain that is worse at night or when lying on the affected shoulder
— Weakness with lifting, reaching, or overhead activity
— A catching, popping, or grinding sensation in the shoulder
— Stiffness or pain that has not improved with rest or physical therapy

Common Questions

Can frozen shoulder or a rotator cuff tear be treated without surgery? +

Yes. Many cases of frozen shoulder and rotator cuff tendinopathy or partial tears respond well to non-operative treatment, including capsular hydrodistension and regenerative injections, helping patients regain motion and avoid surgery.

Why does ultrasound guidance matter? +

Blind injections frequently miss their target. Every procedure at OCMSK is performed under real-time ultrasound guidance, so the diagnosis is confirmed visually and the treatment is delivered to the exact structure causing your pain.

What is capsular hydrodistension? +

Capsular hydrodistension is a minimally invasive, ultrasound-guided procedure that stretches and releases a tight, contracted shoulder joint capsule using a carefully injected fluid mixture — a well-studied treatment for adhesive capsulitis (frozen shoulder).
