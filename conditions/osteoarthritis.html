Diagnosis First

An Accurate Diagnosis Is the Treatment That Matters Most

"Arthritis" is rarely the whole story. Pain around a degenerating joint often involves tendons, ligaments, bursae, or nerves — and treating the wrong structure is why many treatments fail. Every evaluation at OCMSK includes a real-time diagnostic ultrasound exam, so we see exactly what is causing your pain before anything is treated.

We also look at every factor driving your arthritis — prior injuries, joint alignment and mechanics, muscle weakness, activity load, and overall health — and build a plan that addresses the causes, not just the symptoms.

Common signs of osteoarthritis
— Joint pain that worsens with activity and improves with rest
— Morning stiffness that eases within 30 minutes
— Swelling, grinding, or clicking in the knee, hip, or shoulder
— Reduced range of motion or difficulty with stairs and walking
— Pain that has not improved with rest, medication, or physical therapy

Common Questions

Can osteoarthritis really be treated without surgery? +

Yes. Most arthritic joints do not require replacement. Regenerative and neuromodulation therapies target the biology of arthritis to reduce pain, improve function, and in many cases delay or avoid surgery altogether.

Why does ultrasound guidance matter? +

Blind injections frequently miss their target. Every procedure at OCMSK is performed under real-time ultrasound guidance, so the diagnosis is confirmed visually and the treatment is delivered to the exact structure causing your pain.

How is your PRP different? +

We use one of the newest PRP systems available, yielding a higher concentration of platelets and growth factors than conventional PRP, with an activation step that makes growth factors immediately available — the same technology trusted by professional athletes.
