Conditions This Includes

Diagnoses We Commonly Treat

Sacroiliac (SI) Joint Dysfunction

Pain from irritation or instability of the joint connecting the spine and pelvis.

Piriformis Syndrome

Irritation of the sciatic nerve by the piriformis muscle deep in the buttock.

Gluteal Tendinopathy

Degeneration of the tendons at the side of the hip, a common cause of hip pain.

Greater Trochanteric Pain Syndrome

Pain and tenderness over the outside of the hip, often from bursitis or tendinopathy.

Sciatic Nerve Irritation

Irritation of the sciatic nerve causing pain that radiates down the back of the leg.

This list is not exhaustive. We evaluate and treat many other musculoskeletal conditions — contact us to schedule a consultation for a proper diagnosis and treatment options.

Diagnosis First

An Accurate Diagnosis Is the Treatment That Matters Most

Buttock, hip, and low back pain often overlap, and pinpointing the true source — the hip joint, the sacroiliac (SI) joint, the piriformis or gluteal muscles, or an irritated sciatic nerve — is essential to effective treatment. Every evaluation at OCMSK includes a real-time diagnostic ultrasound exam, so we can identify exactly which structure is generating your pain before anything is treated.

Our practice focuses on musculoskeletal causes of buttock, hip, and SI joint pain. We do not perform spinal epidural injections or treat other spine conditions; when an evaluation points to the spine itself, we will refer you to the appropriate specialist.

Common signs of buttock, hip & SI joint pain
— Deep buttock pain that worsens with sitting or climbing stairs
— Pain at the side of the hip that is worse when lying on that side
— Pain that radiates down the back of the leg
— Low back or SI joint pain that is worse with standing from a seated position or one-sided loading
— Pain that has not improved with rest, stretching, or physical therapy

Common Questions

Can hip, buttock, or SI joint pain be treated without surgery? +

Yes. Most cases of hip, buttock, and SI joint pain respond well to accurate diagnosis and targeted, ultrasound-guided treatment, without the need for surgery.

Why does ultrasound guidance matter? +

Blind injections frequently miss their target. Every procedure at OCMSK is performed under real-time ultrasound guidance, so the diagnosis is confirmed visually and the treatment is delivered to the exact structure causing your pain.

Do you perform epidural injections or treat spine conditions? +

No. Our practice focuses on musculoskeletal causes of pain, including the hip, buttock, and sacroiliac (SI) joint. We do not perform spinal epidural injections or treat other spine conditions; if your evaluation points to the spine itself, we will refer you to the appropriate specialist.
