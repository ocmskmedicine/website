Diagnosis First

An Accurate Diagnosis Is the Treatment That Matters Most

Heel pain isn't always plantar fasciitis — it can come from a nerve, a stress reaction, or fat pad irritation, and treating the wrong structure is why many treatments fail. Every evaluation at OCMSK includes a real-time diagnostic ultrasound exam of the foot, so we see exactly what is causing your pain before anything is treated.

We also look at what's driving the strain on your plantar fascia — foot mechanics, footwear, activity load, and calf flexibility — and build a plan that addresses the cause, not just the pain.

Common signs of plantar fasciitis
— Sharp heel pain with the first steps in the morning
— Pain that eases with movement but returns after standing or activity
— Tenderness along the arch or bottom of the heel
— Pain that worsens after long periods of standing or walking
— Heel pain that has not improved with stretching, orthotics, or rest

Common Questions

Can plantar fasciitis be treated without surgery? +

Yes. The great majority of plantar fasciitis cases improve with non-surgical care. Regenerative therapies like PRP target the degenerated tissue directly, helping patients avoid surgical release.

Why does ultrasound guidance matter? +

Blind injections frequently miss their target. Every procedure at OCMSK is performed under real-time ultrasound guidance, so the diagnosis is confirmed visually and the treatment is delivered to the exact structure causing your pain.
