Diagnosis First

An Accurate Diagnosis Is the Treatment That Matters Most

Trigger finger happens when the flexor tendon sheath becomes inflamed and narrowed, catching the tendon as it glides. Every evaluation at OCMSK includes a real-time diagnostic ultrasound exam of the hand, so we can confirm the diagnosis and pinpoint exactly where the tendon is catching before anything is treated.

We also assess how many fingers are involved and how severe the triggering has become, and build a plan matched to your symptoms and goals.

Common signs of trigger finger
— A finger or thumb that catches, clicks, or locks when bending or straightening
— A tender lump or nodule at the base of the finger, in the palm
— Stiffness that is worse in the morning
— A finger that becomes locked in a bent position and must be straightened with the other hand
— Symptoms that have not improved with splinting or rest

Ultrasound-Guided Corticosteroid Injection

A precisely placed injection into the tendon sheath reduces inflammation and allows the tendon to glide freely again — the most effective non-surgical treatment for trigger finger.

✓ Precisely Targeted — Every injection is placed under real-time ultrasound guidance directly into the tendon sheath, not the surrounding tissue.
✓ Fast, In-Office Procedure — The injection takes just minutes, with no incision and minimal downtime.
✓ High Success Rate — Most patients experience resolution of catching or locking after one or two precisely placed injections.
✓ A First-Line Alternative to Surgery — Often avoids the need for a surgical trigger finger release.

Platelet Rich Plasma (PRP)

A regenerative option for recurrent or resistant cases, using your own concentrated platelets and growth factors to support tendon healing.

A precise needle-based procedure to address a persistent, thickened tendon nodule that has not responded to injection.

Common Questions

Can trigger finger be treated without surgery? +

Yes. Most cases of trigger finger resolve with one or a small series of precisely placed, ultrasound-guided injections, without the need for a surgical release.

Why does ultrasound guidance matter? +

Blind injections frequently miss their target. Every procedure at OCMSK is performed under real-time ultrasound guidance, so the diagnosis is confirmed visually and the treatment is delivered to the exact structure causing your pain.

How many injections will I need? +

Many patients see significant improvement after a single ultrasound-guided injection. If triggering persists, a second injection or an alternative regenerative approach may be recommended.
